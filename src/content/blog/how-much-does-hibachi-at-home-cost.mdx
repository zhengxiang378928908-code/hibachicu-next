## What Does Hibachi at Home Typically Cost?

Private hibachi catering usually runs **$50 per adult** and **$25 per child** (ages 3–10). Most services, including Hibachi CU, set a **$500 minimum booking** to cover chef travel, setup, and equipment.

That means a party of 10 adults would start around **$500** — and that includes the full teppanyaki show, fresh-cooked meal, and cleanup.

## What's Included in the Price?

A standard hibachi at home package typically includes:

- **A professional hibachi chef** who brings the grill, tools, and all cooking equipment
- **Full teppanyaki-style performance** — onion volcano, egg tricks, spatula flips
- **A complete meal** with fried rice, mixed vegetables, and your choice of protein (chicken, steak, shrimp, or salmon)
- **Sauces** — yum yum sauce, ginger sauce, soy sauce, and spicy mayo
- **Setup and cleanup** — the chef handles everything from start to finish

## Common Add-Ons and Upgrades

Want to level up your party? Here are typical upgrade costs:

- **Extra protein** (lobster tail, filet mignon): +$10–$15 per person
- **Combination plates** (two proteins): +$10 per person
- **Gyoza or shumai appetizers**: $3–$5 per piece
- **Kids meals**: Usually half the adult price

## What Affects the Final Price?

Several factors can move the total up or down:

- **Guest count** — Larger parties often get a better per-person rate
- **Location** — A travel fee may apply if your event is outside the chef's core service area
- **Menu choices** — Premium proteins like lobster cost more than chicken
- **Day and time** — Weekend evenings during summer book fast and may have higher demand

## How Does This Compare to Dining Out?

A hibachi restaurant dinner for two typically costs **$60–$100+** before tip and drinks. With at-home catering, you're paying a similar per-person rate but getting:

- A private chef cooking just for your group
- No wait times, no crowded dining rooms
- The comfort of your own backyard, patio, or vacation rental
- A full show experience tailored to your party

## Tips to Get the Most Value

1. **Book early** — Popular dates (summer weekends, holidays) fill up weeks in advance
2. **Confirm your guest count** a few days before so you're not paying for no-shows
3. **Keep the menu simple** — One or two proteins keeps costs down without sacrificing quality
4. **Ask about package deals** for larger parties (20+ guests)
