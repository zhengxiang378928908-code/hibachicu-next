## Why Hibachi Is the Perfect Mother's Day Gift

Every year it's the same struggle — try to book a restaurant weeks in advance, wait 45 minutes for a table, sit in a noisy dining room, and split the check at the end. Mom smiles, but it's not exactly relaxing.

A hibachi at home dinner flips the script. A private chef shows up at your house, sets up the grill in the backyard, and cooks a full teppanyaki meal while the whole family watches. Mom doesn't cook, doesn't clean, and doesn't have to get in the car. She just sits back and enjoys.

## What a Mother's Day Hibachi Dinner Looks Like

Here's how the evening typically flows:

1. **The chef arrives 15–20 minutes early** and sets up the grill and prep station
2. **The family gathers around** — drinks in hand, relaxed, no rush
3. **The show starts** — fried rice, vegetables, then everyone's protein of choice
4. **Mom gets the VIP treatment** — her plate cooked first, premium upgrades if you want
5. **Everyone eats together** — hot off the grill, family-style
6. **The chef cleans up** — you don't touch a single dish

Total time: about 60–90 minutes. The rest of the evening is yours.

## Planning Tips

### Book Now, Not Later

Mother's Day is **May 10, 2026**. It's one of the highest-demand weekends for hibachi catering. If you're thinking about it, [book your date now](https://www.hibachicu.com/#booking) — popular time slots (5–7 PM) fill up first.

### Keep It Simple

You don't need to plan a huge event. A Mother's Day hibachi works great with just the immediate family — even 10 people hits the minimum and makes it feel special.

### Set the Atmosphere

The chef handles the food. You handle the vibe:

- **Flowers on the table** — it's Mother's Day, after all
- **A playlist** — something chill in the background before the chef starts
- **String lights** — if you're eating at sunset, they make the backyard feel magical
- **A dessert** — the chef handles dinner, you bring the cake or her favorite treat

### Get the Kids Involved

Let the kids "plan" the dinner for Mom. They pick the menu, help set up chairs, and maybe even make a card. It turns the evening into a family effort instead of just another dinner.
